The Little Red Schoolhouse (LRS) is the University of Chicago's idea of a business composition course. Formally titled "Academic and Professional Writing," it teaches a very pragmatic writing style which prioritizes clarity, relevance, and persuasiveness. I consider it the single most useful course I took at the university, and the LRS Rewrites series is a way for me to keep practicing the lessons learned, by rewriting short pieces into the style taught at the Little Red Schoolhouse.

While the original LRS materials are distributed solely via hard copy at the start of each term, online notes are available for the spin-off programs at Penn State and UVa. The LRS faculty at Chicago also publish a Sentence of the Week series which critiques and rewrites problematic sentences submitted "from the wild."
